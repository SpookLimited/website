Comms room monitoring

Comms rooms (also known as communications rooms) are usually dedicated rooms hosting communication and network equipment. They are designed as distribution hubs for communicating from the main IT room to users within a building. Comms rooms usually have hubs, routers, servers, switches, firewalls, storage devices, telephony, access control and security equipment installed within them.

Spook Comms room monitoring

Spook's remote cloud based monitoring service, called OmniWatch, helps IT managers keep a remote eye on their comms rooms. Because comms rooms often contain less equipment than that of a server room the operating environment can usually be more relaxed albeit still important.

Digital communications and network devices in comms rooms normally do not have as many moving parts to contend with compared to their server room counterparts. As a consequence the climate of a comms room can often run at warmer temperatures and are more power efficient as cooling units run in a less stressed state.

Many comms rooms are distributed away from the main server room and are behind secure doors, therefore remote monitoring is an important function. OmniWatch is designed to alert users to changes in the climate of a comms room that could easily go unnoticed. Key things like temperature or monitoring the air flow of a cooling unit can help protect a comms room and prevent it from facing unplanned downtime.
