Public utility intelligent monitoring

Spook's award winning remote monitoring service called OmniWatch monitors energy consumption and water usage.

Measuring consumables from public utility companies across many different service providers, assets and areas; OmniWatch presents realtime readings in an easy to use real-time digital dashboard.

Public utility monitoring empowers data managers to understand and work towards making consumption as efficient as possible.

The importance of public utility monitoring

Understanding consumption from public utility service organisations is crucial as costs of energy continue to rise and the consumption of water as a finite resource is becoming more scrutinised.

There are many reasons understanding non-domestic utility service usage is important however the most common reasons are:

  • The unit price for energy is volatile and is at the highest unit cost ever recorded. The UK Government have set a temporary price cap for non-domestic energy to £211 per MWh for electricity and £75 per MWh for gas until March 2023.
  • Organisations are under extreme pressure to minimise electricity and gas usage in order to reduce their overall running costs.
  • Public utility firms are likely to introduce off-peak schemes for non-domestic consumers with incentives to switch energy intensive applications from peak to off-peak times where possible.
  • Water efficiency is becoming more important with the rise in heat waves and droughts.
  • Place public utilities service providers under pressure to ensure utility bills are accurate and can be proven and accounted for.

By understanding an organisations consumption from public utility firms means energy managers can use actual data as proof of concept to change usage patterns and understand their most energy hungry times and processes.

By introducing new processes, organisations can start to load balance usage for maximum efficiency gains and lower billing costs.

Public utility remote monitoring key features

Rapid return on investment (ROI)

OmniWatch is an extremely cost efficient monitoring solution.

Because Spook's public utility monitoring bundle uses LoraWAN sensors and cellular gateways to harvest data there is no need to install heavy duty infrastructure in order to achieve a total monitoring solution.

The ROI is rapid and the installation is "process light".

Spook IoT sensors and gateways

Spook wireless sensors arrive pre-configured and calibrated and have on-board on/off switches making installation very easy.

Just install the sensor and switch it on for instant access to OmniWatch, via any internet enabled device.

Wireless cellular and ethernet gateways

Spook recommended gateways come with ethernet and cellular connectivity as standard.

Gateways reside in appropriate locations around the building - dictated by the spread of sensors.

Sensor data is transmitted over the ethernet network with a cellular backhaul and is encrypted end to end and is sent to Spook's OmniWatch servers.

Gateways can be set to cellular operation as priority, ensuring monitoring is independent of the corporate network.

SIM features

Cellular gateways each have a pre-installed data roaming SIM card.

Data roaming

Each SIM is configured to work on all major cellular networks.

Full LTE support

SIMs are capable of utilising 4G, 3G and 2G to ensure there is always a strong signal.

Price included

SIM data charges are already included in Spook's pricing model. No hidden surprises.

BEMS

For organisations that have already installed BEMS (Building Energy Management Systems), OmniWatch works with these too.

So if you already have a BEMS system, why not breath new life into it by implementing OmniWatch to passively harvest the sensor readings and present them onto secure, internet based, digital dashboards, for a modern and functionally rich approach to BEMS management.
