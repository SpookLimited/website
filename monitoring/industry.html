Refrigeration monitoring

Refrigeration is often overlooked as an industry but it spans food processing to drug and medicine production and storage. In terms of controlling the temperature of its contents a refrigeration unit is extremely important. In the medical industry drugs and medicines need to be stored in cool, dry conditions to protect their composition.

Boiler room monitoring

Boilers come in all shapes and sizes but all must maintain a certain temperature level for safe, reliable and effective operations. Boilers typically have four main components; the burner, the combustion chamber, the heat exchanger and the pluming apparatus and they distribute pressurised water around the system. Old or improperly maintained boilers often develop heavy duty leaks due to wear and tear. Most industrial boilers reside in a dedicated room, however this is usually a room where no one goes until there is a problem.

Spook's remote monitoring service called OmniWatch helps by remotely monitoring key areas and sending real-time alarms when there’s a problem.

Plumbing monitoring

Many buildings have extensive plumbing with pressurised water being processed around the facility. Water ingress is one of the most disruptive environmental conditions that can be monitored. This is because the effects of unwanted water are immediate and sometimes long-lasting. Once the cause of water ingress has been addressed the drying out process can be protracted and in some cases dangerous. Temperature is also a key factor to ensure pipework and water tanks are not frozen during winter months. This is particularly challenging for older grade listed or heritage buildings whose plumbing can be many decades old.

Spook's remote monitoring service, called OmniWatch, helps by remotely monitoring key areas and sending real-time alarms providing valuable time to take remedial action.

Veterinary clinic monitoring

Vets premises need to store drugs and medicines securely. Knowing they are stored in adequate environmental conditions to ensure longevity together with ensuring they are locked in appropriate rooms and cabinets is very important. Vets also need to cater for short term animal occupancy for when pets and other creatures need to be operated on or to recuperate. Ensuring these areas do not get too hot and cause heat exhaustion for their occupants is prime concern. Also, when animals are recuperating from an operation they are often sedated and remain motionless until the sedation abates. Being able to monitor motion in a recovery area saves time and effort in staff needing to remember to take a look to check matters whilst they maintain their busy veterinary schedules. Veterinarians working in enclosed spaces in winter months (for example intensive animal production facilities being heated by combustion sources) will most commonly be exposed to carbon monoxide (CO) together with the animals they are treating. CO is colourless and has no odour so monitoring for its presence is crucial.

Spook's remote monitoring service, called OmniWatch, helps by remotely monitoring many aspects of a vets clinic and associated animal welfare compounds by sending real-time alarms for events that require immediate attention.

Data center/server room monitoring

IT departments are often at the vanguard of maximising the uptime of their services to their business, users and customers alike. Typically IT staff are located some distance from the actual data center/server room, often in a different part of the business, or sometimes in a different part of the country or world. Physical visits to the server rooms are therefore rare and normally confined to when on-site work is actually required. This is known as running a lights out operation which is increasingly common. During the Covid-19 crisis of 2020 Spook customer IT rooms were left unattended for over 99% of the time.

OmniWatch is designed to be protocol neutral and works with SNMP, BACnet, Modbus, XML, JSON and others. The ability to monitor 3rd party equipment in harmony with Spook wireless sensors brings a seamless monitoring dynamic to the complicated monitoring needs of facilities departments.

Spook's remote monitoring service, called OmniWatch, helps by remotely monitoring the operating environment of IT rooms and sending real-time alarms for events that require immediate attention.

Hard facilities monitoring

Both facility management and IT departments share similar concerns regarding the monitoring of physical equipment and their general environment. The topic of monitoring systems across the entire facility means the net of equipment that can be monitored is varied and wide. It's important to have a monitoring solution that is meaningful and uncomplicated.

Hard facility services are ones which relate to the physical fabric of the building and cannot easily be removed. They ensure the safety and welfare of employees and generally are required by law.

Spook's remote monitoring service, called OmniWatch, not only works with Spook's range of wireless sensors, it also works with your existing equipment already installed in the facility.

OmniWatch is designed to be protocol neutral and works with SNMP, BACnet, Modbus, XML, JSON and others. The ability to monitor 3rd party equipment in harmony with Spook wireless sensors brings a hassle free solution to monitoring the complicated needs of facilities departments.

NHS and professional healthcare

The NHS stands for the National Health Service. The NHS provides 'free at the point of use or delivery'. This means that any UK resident can see a doctor and get diagnosis or treatment for an illness without charge for this service during or after the visit. There are just over 220 NHS trusts across the UK and the NHS is the single biggest employer across the country.

Information Communications Technology (ICT) is a core component of the smooth running of the NHS. The digital world means technology can support the delivery of health care in a number of areas from providing and storing information and advice to administration and transactions. Moving forward, IT is proving invaluable in assisting diagnosis and even surgery. With ICT being so intrinsic to the NHS the rooms supporting IT systems, ranging from data centers, server rooms, comms rooms and more, have never been so critical to supporting the professional healthcare industry.

Spook's remote monitoring service, called OmniWatch, helps by remotely monitoring key IT areas and sending real-time alarms to events that need immediate attention.
