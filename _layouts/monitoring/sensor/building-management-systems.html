What is a Building Management System (BMS)?

A BMS is a centralised computer-based controller and monitoring system for a building or facility.

The most common use of BMS is to control a buildings mechanical and electrical equipment such as heating ventilation and air conditioning (HVAC), lighting and security systems.

BMSs vary and usually include sensors, pressure switches, flow meters, controllers, and relays.

Benefits of building management systems

Building management has seen an increasing emphasis on energy efficiency and green building initiatives. BMSs help building or facility managers achieve standards and provide evidential support for legislation.

At the same time, IT integration and the increasing use of analytics and actionable data has led to BMSs being placed at the heart of building design strategy.

Power monitoring

A BMS may also be used to monitor and control power distribution, energy consumption and uninterrupted power supplies (UPS) and may be referred to as building energy management systems (BEMS).

Building power can be easily monitored through a BMS and OmniWatch and can have the added benefit of, through the increase of data availability and features, load balancing power consumption and impact testing for changes from a single room to an entire building.

OmniWatch also supports calculations for site wide PUE and DCiE which can be used to find inefficiencies and help make the necessary changes to improve performance or reduce cost/demand of air conditioning and lighting when not needed.
