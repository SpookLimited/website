Automated alerting

Generators provide a reliable source of back-up emergency power for long periods of time. Due to how a generator is used it is more often than not installed outside or in an area that is not normally accessed by staff.

By monitoring the generator you can be instantly alerted to any change in its operating state or detect mechanical issues that need addressing before they fail.

Using OmniWatch's 3-tier alarm escalation process ensure the right personnel is made aware of events and receive alerts via email, SMS and phone call via our UK call centre.

Avoiding potential damage

One of the most common causes of damage to generators is when they're under-loaded. That is, generators function most effectively when they're operating at high capacity. When they're used for low capacity tasks (such as powering 10% of the voltage load), they can start to build up carbon and internal glazing.

If this continues, eventually, soot and residue from unused fuel can accumulate and clog the generator's piston rings.

The longer this happens, the more pronounced the degradation of the diesel generator’s system. To avoid this, generators should have a capacity at approximately 70% of the maximum load.
