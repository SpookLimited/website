iPDU Global and socket level monitoring

Power management is crucial within server rooms and data centers. For the best financial and computing results, the modern data center manager needs an efficient power monitoring strategy in order to avoid server downtime and optimise power usage.

With this in mind, iPDU units are crucial. Rack mounted iPDU units provide energy from the utility or UPS power supply to servers and other electronic assets, and monitoring the power metrics at global or individual socket level can be a revelation.

By monitoring iPDU power metrics in realtime means potential problems can be detected before they have negative consequences.

Load balancing

By measuring the power consumption of an iPDU over time the trend data of its power metrics becomes a valuable commodity. Not only can any patterns in power spikes be identified but identification of how high they reach when servers are at maximum stress levels can prove invaluable for building in power related latency.

Ensuring efficient load balancing reduces these stresses and helps power managers to intelligently plan for load balancing it order to spread the consumption of assets across the iPDU estate.

Temperature

Servers in general consume power and release it as heat, but when there are large variances in consumption because of workloads, the heat released from IT equipment also rises.

Fluctuations in server power consumption can cause dangerous increases in temperature, creating unplanned heat spots. While data centres have cooling systems, they may not be designed to handle specific localised hot spots. As temperature rises, equipment is likely to auto-shut down or act abnormally.

iPDU units usually have onboard temperature sensors, this is perfect to monitor for localised, hard to find, hot spots.

Stranded power

Stranded power is the spare power available but not used on a power strip. This may be small on a single unit but extrapolating this across hundreds of power strips in an installation can provide valuable spare power for new projects before having to extend/expand the installation with more PDUs.

Stranded power can be calculated by monitoring the global power consumption of an iPDU versus the nameplate power (16amp/32amp) of the device.

Spook Smart Sensors calculate stranded power in realtime.
