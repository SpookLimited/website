Cellular monitoring

Keeping remote monitoring secure and independent

{% include header-buttons.html %}

Spook's cellular router solution won the recent 2020 CIR Business Continuity Awards category of "Specialist Technology Company of the Year".

Unlike a standard router that accesses the internet through a cable, a cellular router enables you to get internet access through a cellular data network. This is perfect for keeping remote monitoring independent of a customers network or for areas where broadband is not an option.

Data Roaming SIM technology

Spook includes as standard a data roaming sim at no cost for usage. The sim has over 80 service providers including O2, Three, Vodafone and EE.

Data resilience

By utilising machine-to-machine (M2M) data only SIMs within Spook's cellular routers the solution is able to operate in areas of poor signal and can communicate readings to Spook's servers even when signal is low or even in places where only 2G is available.

For areas where there is no signal, for example; server rooms in basements, external aerials can be installed or the cellular router can be installed anywhere within a customers facility. So in this example the cellular router can be installed in a different part of the building, away from the actual monitored area.

Try before you buy

Spook can provide a loan cellular router to try before you decide to buy one. This ensures the cellular router proves its ability to work before you buy it.

Contingency

Spook sets the hunt for signal strength to every 24 hours so if a new mobile service provider pops up in the vicinity with a stronger signal, it is selected automatically.

Also, If the mobile service provider has a sudden loss of service Spook has designed a handy feature to auto-hunt for an alternative service provider within ten minutes of loss of signal. This ensures remote monitoring contingency is automatically achieved.

Monitoring

Spook's standard cellular router comes with 4 x LAN ports as standard. In order to remotely monitor equipment simply connect the device that requires monitoring into one of the available on-board LAN ports.

For organisations that require more than 4 connections, simply plug a multi-port switch to one of the on board LAN ports to achieve extend monitoring.

Plug and play

Spook pre-configures the cellular router to work with our award winning cloud based monitoring service called OmniWatch. So simply provide power and turn on the cellular router for instant access to your OmniWatch real time dashboards.
