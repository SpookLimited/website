Omniwatch dashboards are designed to provide users instant access to their most important information

Spook's aim is to ensure that users are not left in the dark about the health and stability of their business and put that data straight into their hands. Singular acccess point

OmniWatch is capable of bringing existing monitoring installations and various equipment types together into a single application, no longer making the user reliant on countless different manufacturer interfaces locked to the local network.

Spook OmniWatch is compatible with a large number of hardware manufacturers ranging from household names to small independent hardware producers and are constantly looking to add more over time.

Spook also supports multiple protocols such as SNMP, JSON, BACnet, XML and Modbus. By not relying on a singular protocol, unlike other solutions, OmniWatch can seamlessly present real time readings and compare data from multiple pieces of equipment regardless of manufacturer simply and easily.

Access controls

The areas that OmniWatch traditionally monitors are often spread across several departments that contain disparate equipment. Each department usually have their own priorities and when trying to monitor equipment on a piecemeal basis can be difficult to manage or keep secure. For example a refrigeration alarm may need to inform both the maintenance/service company and those that use the equipment on site whereas a water alert in a server room may need to be handled by the facilities department instead of the IT department. Granting and managing access rights to multiple users in such cases are time consuming and potentially expose users to information they either don't need or shouldn't see.

OmniWatch solves this issue and streamlines the workflow for the end users with its built in access controls. The controls are flexible and can be assigned to individual sensors and equipment types.

Custom sensor settings

Spook recognises similar sensors may need different settings dependent where they are installed. For example a temperature sensor near an air conditioning unit may need lower variables when compared to a similar sensor located near to a heat source.

All sensor settings are variable and can be easily changed via the individual sensor interface within OmniWatch.

Automatically generated alerts based on user set trip values

When sensor readings reach the set alarm levels they visually change for easy visual identification when viewing the real-time dashboard. Once an alert is broadcast the offending sensors invoke Spook's emergency alarm escalation procedure.
